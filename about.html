I’m a giver. I’m rather obsessed with making people happy — and that mindset naturally extends to my work.

I grew up in a rural town in South Korea, exploring rivers and mountains. As a kid, my passion was sports — football, skiing, swimming, boxing, and more. Football especially instilled in me a sense of leadership, teamwork, and discipline. Everyone around me thought I’d become an athlete one day. For my first major, I chose Nursing because I loved the idea of helping people in need. Even now, that same motivation drives everything I do.
‍
Later, I found another way to help people — through technology. I switched my major to Data Science, where I discovered how data can uncover human patterns and behaviours.

During my studies in Data Science, I began to see data as a way to understand people — their needs, their emotions, their experiences. I realised that behind every dataset, there are human stories waiting to be heard. That understanding drew me to design, where I could turn insight into impact and empathy into experience.

Today, I design with both empathy and evidence — combining human insight with data-driven thinking to create experiences that are intuitive, meaningful, and inclusive. I see design as a bridge between people and technology, and I’m passionate about crafting solutions that not only look good, but genuinely make life better.
